# 中文版（LinkedIn 主貼文）

我做資安 SI 18 年，2026 年換了一個賣法

18 年前我入行時，資安 SI 賣的東西很簡單：

防火牆、IPS、SIEM、WAF、PAM。

每個產品都對應一個明確威脅，每個威脅都對應一個合規條款。客戶的問題是「會不會被駭、會不會被罰」。我的答案是「裝這幾台，給你過 ISO」。

這個賣法，跟了我 15 年。

直到 2025 年第三季，我發現賣不動了。

不是客戶沒預算。是客戶的問題變了。

「我們在試 ChatGPT 接內部資料。資安怎麼弄？」
「業務想做 AI 客服 Agent，可以給它打到客戶資料庫嗎？」
「總部要我們導入 Copilot，但法遵說會違反 GDPR，怎麼解？」

這些問題，我過去 18 年的工具箱沒有一個答案。

更尷尬的：客戶問完，下一句通常是「我們先試試看 Microsoft / Google 自己給的方案」。

這就是 2025 下半年的真相——當 AI 把資安問題從「網路邊界」變成「員工行為」，亞洲資安 SI 過去 18 年的資產，從『護城河』變成『遺產』。

我花了 6 個月看清這件事。

然後我換了一個賣法。

過去賣的是：圍住公司網路。
現在賣的是：讓客戶的 AI 員工敢上線。

過去 deliver 的是：設備清單 + 規則設定。
現在 deliver 的是：Agent 治理方法論 + 在地法規對照 + 紅隊測試報告。

過去客戶痛點是：怕被駭。
現在客戶痛點是：怕 AI 出包後沒人扛。

換完之後，前 3 個月很痛。老客戶聽不懂新詞，新客戶覺得「這又不是資安」。但 2026 Q1 開始，提案的成功率明顯回升——因為我變成全市場少數幾家真的在解新問題的人。

寫給亞洲同行的 3 個建議：

第一，停止只賣「防」。

「敢」才是 AI 時代的關鍵字。客戶要的是敢上線、敢被稽核、敢交給董事會。

第二，把過去的工具箱當原料，不要當成品。

18 年累積的 SIEM、PAM、紅隊經驗都是底氣，但組合方式必須重做。

第三，方法論先行，工具後面跟。

Credo AI、Lakera、Cisco 的工具會持續演化，但「這套東西怎麼合台灣 / 韓國 / 日本法規上線」的方法論，是我們亞洲 SI 的真正護城河。

18 年的資產，2026 年要決定它是護城河還是包袱。

你的決定是？

# English Version (LinkedIn cross-post)

18 Years in Security SI, and in 2026 I Changed What I Sell

18 years ago when I started, security SI sold simple things:

Firewalls. IPS. SIEM. WAF. PAM.

Each product mapped to a clear threat, each threat mapped to a compliance clause. The customer's question was "will I get breached, will I get fined?" My answer was "install these boxes, you'll pass ISO."

That selling motion carried me for 15 years.

Until Q3 2025, when it stopped working.

Not because customers ran out of budget. Because their questions changed.

"We're piloting ChatGPT against internal data. How do we secure it?"
"Sales wants an AI customer-service agent — can we let it hit the customer database?"
"HQ wants Copilot rolled out, but legal says it'll break GDPR. How do we solve this?"

For these questions, my 18-year toolbox had zero answers.

Worse: after asking, the customer's next sentence was usually "we'll just try whatever Microsoft or Google bundles."

That was the truth of late 2025 — when AI moved the security problem from "network boundary" to "employee behavior," Asian security SI's 18-year asset stack stopped being a moat and started being a legacy.

It took me six months to see this clearly.

Then I changed what I sell.

I used to sell: fences around the corporate network.
Now I sell: letting your AI employees go live with confidence.

I used to deliver: equipment lists and rule configurations.
Now I deliver: agent governance methodology + local regulatory mapping + red team test reports.

The old customer pain was: fear of being breached.
The new customer pain is: fear that no one will own it when AI screws up.

The first three months after the pivot were painful. Old customers didn't follow the new vocabulary, new customers said "this isn't security." But starting Q1 2026, my proposal win rate is visibly recovering — because I'm one of the few in the market actually solving the new problem.

Three pieces of advice for Asian peers:

First, stop selling "defense" alone.

"Confidence to deploy" is the keyword of the AI era. Customers want to go live, get audited, and present to the board with confidence.

Second, treat your old toolbox as raw material, not finished product.

18 years of SIEM, PAM, and red team experience is your foundation, but the combinations have to be rebuilt.

Third, methodology first, tools second.

Credo AI, Lakera, and Cisco tools will keep evolving. But "how do we deploy this in compliance with Taiwanese / Korean / Japanese law" is the actual moat for Asian SIs.

18 years of asset — in 2026 you decide whether it's a moat or a millstone.

What's your decision?
