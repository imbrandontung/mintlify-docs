# 中文版（LinkedIn 主貼文）

兩筆併購，亞洲資安 SI 的真正威脅不是它們

過去 12 個月，兩筆併購安靜發生：

Cisco 吃下 Robust Intelligence。
Palo Alto 吃下 Protect AI。

兩家美國資安巨頭，把 AI 紅隊測試與 ML 供應鏈安全，從「獨立小廠生意」升格成「企業資安標配模組」。

我看完 NVIDIA GTC 2026 的 126 場 sessions，要對亞洲的資安 SI 同行說一句話：

威脅不是這兩家被吃掉。威脅是「方法論」被吃掉了。

過去 5 年，AI 資安在亞洲市場長這樣：客戶問「LLM 安全怎麼搞」，我們把 Lakera、HiddenLayer、Robust Intelligence 三家湊個 demo，順便加自家紅隊服務，一單做下來。

這個生意模式，從這兩筆併購開始失效。

當 AI 紅隊變成 Cisco / Palo Alto 套餐裡的一個 SKU，亞洲 SI 的議價空間從「我幫你選工具 + 做服務」壓縮成「我幫你導入大廠標準功能」。利潤被擠壓，差異化被稀釋。

那真正的機會在哪？

我的判斷是這 3 件事：

第一，在地法規層。Credo AI、Holistic AI 的全球框架到現在還是英文＋歐美法規邏輯。台灣 PDPA、行政院生成式 AI 指引、韓國 AI 倫理基準、日本 APPI、中國生成式 AI 暫行管理辦法——這 5 套法規沒有一家國際大廠原生支援。這格亞洲 SI 還來得及佔。

第二，方法論層。NVIDIA 自家 IT 用 Agentic AI 跑晶片設計、IT 維運、供應鏈、員工生產力四個 domain，整套 reference architecture 已經公開。亞洲 SI 真正的差異化，是「把 reference 翻成台灣金融業 / 製造業 / 醫療業可落地的 SOP」。

第三，紅隊服務化。當紅隊工具被大廠收編，紅隊專家工時反而稀缺。把 NVIDIA Garak、Microsoft PyRIT 兩個開源工具包進來，賣的是專家天數，不是授權費。

亞洲資安 SI 接下來 24 個月最大的選擇題：要繼續代理工具，還是把工具當原料、把方法論當主菜？

我選後者。

你的選擇是？

# English Version (LinkedIn cross-post)

Two Acquisitions, and Why They're Not the Real Threat to Asia's Security SIs

Two acquisitions, quietly, in the past 12 months:

Cisco bought Robust Intelligence.
Palo Alto bought Protect AI.

Two US security giants just promoted AI red teaming and ML supply chain security from a niche startup play into a standard module inside enterprise security suites.

After watching all 126 sessions at NVIDIA GTC 2026, here's my one-sentence message to fellow security SIs in Asia:

The threat isn't that these two got acquired. The threat is that the methodology got acquired with them.

For the past five years, AI security in the Asian market looked like this: a client asked "how do we secure LLMs?", we stitched together demos from Lakera, HiddenLayer, and Robust Intelligence, added our own red team service, and closed the deal.

That model starts breaking the day AI red teaming becomes a SKU inside a Cisco or Palo Alto bundle. The Asian SI's negotiating room shrinks from "I'll pick your tools and run your service" to "I'll deploy a major vendor's standard features." Margins squeeze. Differentiation evaporates.

So where does the real opportunity sit?

Three places, in my view:

First, the local regulation layer. Credo AI's and Holistic AI's frameworks are still English-first and Western-regulation-first. Taiwan's PDPA, the Executive Yuan's GenAI guidelines, Korea's AI ethics standards, Japan's APPI, China's interim rules on generative AI — not one global vendor supports those five regulatory regimes natively. Asian SIs can still claim this ground.

Second, the methodology layer. NVIDIA's own IT runs Agentic AI across four domains: chip design, IT operations, supply chain, and workforce productivity. The reference architecture is public. The Asian SI's real differentiation is translating that reference into deployable SOPs for Taiwan's banks, manufacturers, and hospitals.

Third, productizing the red team. When red team tools get absorbed by giants, red team expert hours become the scarce resource. Wrap NVIDIA Garak and Microsoft PyRIT, sell expert days, not licenses.

The biggest decision Asian security SIs face over the next 24 months: keep reselling tools, or treat tools as raw material and methodology as the main course?

I'm choosing the latter.

What about you?
